Buylettin

Build On, ASEAN 2021 Finalist

Background

Build On, ASEAN 2021 is a competitive hackathon hosted by Amazon Web Services (AWS) that challenges students to create and innovate technological solutions to address challenge statements faced by the sponsors. My team decided to take on the challenge statement proposed by Carousell, which is "how might we encourage more people to buy and sell pre-owned items?". The competition consisted of two stages: a proposal submission stage and a final presentation for shortlisted proposals. Our proposal was shortlisted and we were given two weeks to create a prototype of our solution.

Problem Statement

The challenge statement we chose to address is "how might we encourage more people to buy and sell pre-owned items?". We felt that current secondhand C2C e-commerce marketplaces are largely one-sided and comes with various inconveniences that makes it a hassle for both buyers and sellers, thus turning them away from engaging in buying and selling secondhand products. Sellers may be hesitant to sell their items as they are unsure of the demand of such an item, while buyers are only limited to the items that sellers have posted on the platform. It is important to encourage the exchange of pre-owned items as it contributes to waste reduction and promotes local economic growth.

Solution Overview

Our proposed platform, Buylettin, introduces a Buyer's Listing / Request Board feature where buyers can list the items that they are looking to buy. With this request board, users can list items of interest and the description of the criteria that they are looking for. Keeping the traditional seller-side listings as well, a recommendation system that takes into consideration the similarity of listing keywords and user interactions will be utilized to connect both sides. Sellers will be recommended buyers' listings that are similar to an item that they have posted, and buyers will be recommended sellers' listings that are similar to the requests that they have put up. That way, C2C e-commerce becomes a two-way process: buyers do not necessarily have to be limited by listings that are already available and sellers are able to actively search for buyers.

In addition, we have a public QnA system, where buyers can post questions about a product that is being sold, the answers to which will be made public. Buyers can also indicate questions on their request listings that they would like all sellers to answer when proposing a deal to them. This feature helps to streamline communication and minimize unnecessary repetitions of information between buyers and sellers.

Market Research

Buyer listings are already a feature of several other C2C e-commerce platforms, such as Carousell, Gumtree, and other online classifieds platforms. However, those features are usually sidelined and sometimes jumbled, with multiple categories being mixed together. We believe that by giving this feature more emphasis, we can fully utilize the idea of letting potential suppliers be aware of the presence of a demand to start drawing them into the market. We would also like to augment our solution with a specific recommendation system that is tailored specifically to it. Moreover, because this is already a familiar concept to users, it is easier for users to adopt, bypassing the general consumers' resistance to change.

Prototype

For this competition, we created a functional prototype deployed on Amazon Cloud. My role in this project was primarily full-stack development, though I was also in charge of DevOps. The frontend was coded using React.js and the backend was coded using Spring Boot.

We deployed the prototype as a simple web application, using Amazon Elastic Beanstalk to help us set up the backend environment and deploying the frontend application as a static web in Amazon S3. We hosted our MySQL database in Amazon RDS. We also utilized Amazon Personalize for the recommendation system that aims to connect seller listings with buyer listings. Although this architecture is simple, we felt that it is sufficient for a prototype.

Results

Although we were shortlisted into the finals stage, we unfortunately did not win any prize. Upon reflecting upon this, I came to the conclusion that we were too focused on the engineering of the prototype that we failed to flesh out our idea, leading to the foundation upon which we built our product to be faulty. We spent most of our time trying to create a fully functional prototype, instead of simply creating just enough to showcase our features and better refine the idea behind it. As such, we were lacking in terms of solution impact and business viability, such as monetization. Apart from that, we had taken a very detached approach to the ideation of our product, failing to conduct enough primary research and empathizing with the would-be users of the product. However, these failings are a reward in its own right. Learning from my mistakes and the successes of the other teams, I am determined to go on to improve in my next endeavour!
