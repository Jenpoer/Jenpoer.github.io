Edugether

1st Place Winner of TCS Sustainathon 2021/22

Background

TCS Sustainathon is an annual competition hosted by Tata Consultancy Services aimed to encourage students to come up with technological innovations to solve real-world issues. TCS Sustainathon Singapore 2021's challenge theme is reimagining education, where participants can choose to address one of three specific challenge statements. Together with my friends, we chose to address the challenge statement "enhancing learners' experience in a blended learning environment". Having started a new chapter of our education during the Covid-19 pandemic, we deeply empathized with the problems caused by the sudden transition to blended learning, and sought to craft a solution for this.

Problem Statement

The challenge statement we chose to address is "enhancing learners' experience in a blended learning environment". Specifically, we decided to tackle the problem of the lack of human interaction caused by the sudden transition to a virtual learning environment. Seeing the dire lack of human interaction as a major hindrance in online learning, we hypothesized that encouraging peer learning and forging a community through an online platform will help to mitigate its negative effects.

User Research

We sent out a survey to approximately 30 university students to validate our hypothesis and get an idea of the needs of our target audience. 71% felt a lack of social interaction in online learning, with 44% feeling that it was very difficult to make new friends in this environment. Despite this, 75% required help from their peers for school work and 53% had to turn to self-sourcing from the Internet.

Market Research

Currently, other edtech platforms like Brainly and Course Hero focus on providing convenient access to study materials in the form of a crowdsourced QnA forum and notes / study documents respectively. Another platform that we felt might help students look for student tutors online is Knack. However, all of these platforms are disjointed, making it difficult for students to keep track of them. We also felt that there is a lack of local community building in these platforms, which is something that we would like to focus more on for our solution.

Solution Overview

Therefore, we proposed Edugether, an online peer-learning platform targeted towards university students, with gamified elements as incentives to help students get access to the study materials they need and connect students with their peers. There are 4 core features in our application:

  • Notes Marketplace: Marketplace where students can sell and purchase crowdsourced notes.
  • Find a Study Group: A platform for students to host and join study groups where they can meet new people.
  • QnA Forum: A forum where students can learn together by asking and answering each others' questions.
  • Tutor-Tutee Matching: A platform where students can advertise their tuition services or look for student tutors willing to offer them tuition with ease.

Gamification

To incentivize participation, we have coins (virtual currency) and reputation system. Using coins, users can buy study materials from the Notes Marketplace. Users can earn coins through active participation on the site, such as by posting in the forum, uploading their own study materials in the marketplace, or hosting/joining group study sessions. Users can also exchange the coins for rewards with real monetary value, such as vouchers. To allow users to gauge the quality of the content posted by other users, each user has a reputation level that is determined by how other users rate their contributions. The higher their reputation, the more rewards that they will be able to earn (e.g. their products will be priced higher in the marketplace).

Machine Learning

Edugether will also leverage machine learning technologies to streamline user experience. Using a recommendation system, we can deliver a more personalized experience that eases the users' process in finding what they're loooking for. We will also use NLP in moderation to help student moderators direct their attention to content flagged as inappropriate and ensure the quality posted on the site.

Results

Our solution was awarded the first place winner of TCS Sustainathon 2021/22 Singapore Finals! It was an incredible honor to be able to present our solution to an esteemed panel of judges and the Guest of Honour, President Halimah Yacob. Seeing the other competitors' creative solutions also inspired me. Having come from a very technical background, this competition challenged me to think of aspects other than technological viability, such as business feasibility (monetization, long-term sustainability, competitive advantages, etc.) and social impact.
